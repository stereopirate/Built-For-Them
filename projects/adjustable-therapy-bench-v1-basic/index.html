Why this version exists

This V1 Basic version replaces routed slot channels with simple drilled holes. The goal is a bench that more parents can build with tools they likely already own — a circular saw or jigsaw, a drill, sandpaper, and common hardware — no router or slot-cutting required.

Important safety note

This is a DIY woodworking plan, not medical advice or a certified medical device. Ask your child's therapist what height and angle are appropriate before use.

  • Use only with direct adult supervision.
  • Confirm height, tilt, and posture with a PT, OT, or qualified clinician before use.
  • Designed for level positions or a gentle one-row tilt only — never force a bigger adjustment.
  • Inspect all four knobs, bolts, and edges before every session.

Full build plan

Overview

This bench supports floor-sitting work and therapy-style activities for children who benefit from a firm, adjustable-height surface. The outside legs slide onto captured bolts in the inside legs — matching hole rows give a level bench, and adjacent rows give a gentle tilt.

Project details

Finished size: Approx. 24 in L x 10 in W, 5 in base leg height, with 4 drilled height positions

Difficulty: Beginner-friendly woodworking

Estimated cost: Approx. $30-$60 depending on hardware and finish

Estimated time: An afternoon, plus finish drying time

Tools

  • Circular saw, jigsaw, miter saw, or handsaw
  • Drill/driver + 5/16 in drill bit
  • Clamps, square, tape measure, pencil
  • Sander or sanding block

No router and no slot channels — every adjustment hole is drilled, which is what makes this version easier for a first-time builder.

Cut list

PartQtySizeNotes
Top124 in x 10 inMain bench surface
Inside leg210 in x 5 inFixed under the top; contains the captured bolts
Outside leg212 in base x 5 in tall, tapered to 10 in topAdjustable outer support; drill two columns of holes
Triangle brace45 in x 4 in right triangleInstalled on the outside of each top/inside-leg joint

All parts nest onto one 24 in x 24 in sheet: cut the top first, rip two 5 in strips for the legs, then a 4 in strip for the triangle braces. Outside legs are symmetrical trapezoids — stack both blanks and cut them together so they match.

Drill-hole adjustment layout

Each inside leg gets two fixed bolt holes near the bottom — once bolted through with washers and lock nuts, these act as semi-permanent studs. Each outside leg gets a front and rear column of four holes, spaced 4 in apart and 3 in in from each edge of the 10 in reference. Drill 5/16 in holes for the 1/4 in bolts so alignment stays forgiving for a first build. Mark and drill one outside leg first, then use it as the template for the second so both sides match. Use the same row in both columns for a level bench, or one adjacent row of difference for a gentle tilt — don't skip more than one row, or the bolts won't line up and the bench can become unstable.

Assembly steps

Sand and round all parts

Sand faces, break every edge, and round the top corners before final assembly.

Attach inside legs to the top

Turn the top upside down. Glue and screw one 10 in x 5 in inside leg under each end of the top. Keep the legs square and centered across the 10 in width.

Add triangle braces

Attach two braces at each inside leg — one on each outside face of the top/leg joint. Use glue plus screws or brad nails.

Capture the bolts in the inside legs

Insert two bolts through each inside leg from the inside toward the outside. Add washers and lock nuts so the bolts act like semi-permanent studs.

Install outside legs

Slide each outside leg onto the exposed bolts at the desired hole row. Add a washer and star knob on the outside. Tighten by hand.

Check stability before use

Set the bench on a flat floor. Check for rocking, loose knobs, sharp edges, splinters, or cracks before every session.

Final inspection checklist

  • All edges, holes, and corners are sanded smooth with no splinters.
  • All four knobs are tight in the chosen hole row.
  • Any tilt uses only one adjacent row of difference — never more.
  • Bench sits flat with no rocking or wobble.
  • Finish has cured fully before use.
  • Adult supervision and a clinician's fit/angle check are in place.
