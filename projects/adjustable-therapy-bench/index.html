Full build plan

Overview

This adjustable therapy bench is built from 3/4 in birch plywood with slotted side plates and knob hardware so the platform can be set flat or at a gentle incline.

Project details

Finished size: Approx. 24 in L x 10 in W x 7 in H, with multiple incline positions

Difficulty: Intermediate woodworking

Estimated cost: Approx. $35-$75 depending on hardware and cushion materials

Estimated time: One weekend, plus finish drying time

Slot pattern guidance

The outer adjustment plates use one vertical channel and several horizontal slots. Drill clean rounded ends first, then connect the openings. The bolts should slide into each position without binding, but the knobs should lock the bench firmly when tightened.

Assembly steps

Confirm the size and use case

This bench is sized as a small therapy/positioning bench. Before building, confirm the child’s current positioning needs with a PT or OT and decide whether you want a bare wood top, removable cushion, or washable cover.

Cut the top platform

Cut the 24 in x 10 in top from 3/4 in birch plywood. Ease every edge with sandpaper or a roundover bit. The top should feel smooth with no sharp corners.

Cut the inner support legs

Cut two 10 in x 7 in inner legs. These sit under the platform and carry the bench load. Drill clearance holes for the adjustment bolts so the bolt path is square and repeatable.

Cut the outer adjustment plates

Cut two outer trapezoid plates using the template dimensions. Lay out the vertical channel and horizontal adjustment slots before cutting. Keep both plates stacked or clamped together while marking so the slots line up.

Create the adjustment slots

Drill relief holes at the slot ends, then connect them with a jigsaw or router. Clean the slots with a file or sandpaper so the bolts slide freely without catching.

Dry fit before glue or finish

Assemble the top, inner legs, outer plates, bolts, washers, and knobs without finish. Test every incline position. The top should lock firmly without side-to-side wobble.

Attach support legs and blocking

Attach the inner support legs square to the underside of the platform. Use glue and screws, pocket holes, or countersunk fasteners. Keep fasteners flush or hidden so nothing scratches the child or floor.

Sand and round over

Sand all exposed faces and plywood edges through 180 or 220 grit. Pay special attention to the adjustment slots, lower corners, and any place a hand may touch.

Finish safely

Apply a water-based polyurethane or child-safe finish to all sides, edges, and inside slot surfaces. Let it cure fully based on the product label before use.

Add the optional cushion

If padding is needed, add a removable cushion made from high-density foam and washable fabric or vinyl. Avoid permanent upholstery until you know the height and angle work well.

Final safety check

Before each use, confirm all knobs are tight, the bench does not rock, the top is not cracked, and the child is supervised. Stop using it if any hardware loosens or wood shows damage.

Final inspection checklist

  • All edges and corners are rounded smooth.
  • Adjustment slots are clean and bolts slide freely.
  • Knobs tighten securely in every incline position.
  • Bench does not wobble or rack side-to-side.
  • Finish has cured fully before use.
  • Cushion is secure but removable for cleaning.
  • Adult supervision and clinical fit check are in place.
