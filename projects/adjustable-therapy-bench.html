Adjustable Therapy Bench

A height-adjustable floor-sitting bench built from one small sheet of plywood, with independent front and rear adjustment for a flat or tilted surface.

Why we built it

This bench was designed by a parent for floor-sitting work and home therapy practice. The goal was a practical alternative that could be built in one afternoon from common materials rather than purchasing a much more expensive commercial bench.

Family-built, not a clinical recommendation

These plans are an educational starting point shared by a family in the community. Ask your child’s therapist whether this type of bench, its height, and any tilt angle are appropriate. Use only with direct adult supervision.

How the adjustment works

Each outer side panel has two columns of five horizontal slots. Four hand-tightened star knobs secure the inner legs at heights from 5 to 9 inches in 1-inch increments. The front and rear columns adjust independently, allowing the top to remain flat or tilt forward or backward.

  • Five tool-free height positions
  • Independent front and rear adjustment
  • Symmetric tapered outer legs for a wider base
  • Removable 1-inch padded top with wipe-clean vinyl base

Materials at a glance

Wood and finish

  • 2 × 2-foot sheet of 3/4-inch Baltic birch plywood
  • Additional plywood for padded-top base
  • Water-based polyurethane
  • 80, 120, and 220 grit sandpaper

Hardware and padding

  • Four 1/4-20 star knobs and 2-inch hex bolts
  • Flat and lock washers
  • 1-inch upholstery foam
  • Vinyl, washable cover fabric, and staples

Before every use

  • Confirm all four knobs are secure and the bolts are seated correctly.
  • Check for splinters, cracks, rocking, loose screws, or damaged hardware.
  • Stop using the bench immediately if any part is damaged or unstable.
  • Keep the child directly supervised and follow therapist guidance.
