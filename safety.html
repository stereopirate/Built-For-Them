Safety notice

Plans are starting points, not prescriptions.

Adaptive equipment affects positioning, movement, access, comfort, and sometimes injury risk. Use careful professional judgment.

Important: Built For Them provides educational information only. Nothing on this site is medical advice, a medical device certification, or a guarantee of safety or suitability.

Before building

  • Discuss the idea with the child’s qualified physical therapist, occupational therapist, physician, or other appropriate clinician.
  • Confirm measurements, intended activity, required support, weight limits, pinch points, fall risks, and supervision needs.
  • Choose materials and hardware appropriate for the expected loads and environment.

Before every use

  • Inspect for loose fasteners, cracks, sharp edges, splinters, worn straps, instability, and damaged components.
  • Place equipment on a stable surface and use only as intended.
  • Provide direct adult supervision.
  • Stop immediately if there is pain, skin irritation, distress, unsafe positioning, instability, or unexpected movement.

Builder responsibility

The builder and user are responsible for evaluating the finished item. Local rules, professional guidance, material quality, workmanship, and individual needs can all affect safety.
