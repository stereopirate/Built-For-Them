Why this exists

A therapy bench that costs $300 shouldn't. Ours costs $50 to build.

My son has Cerebral Palsy. When his therapist recommended a height-adjustable therapy bench for home practice, I looked up the commercial version: $300-400, often not covered by insurance. So I built one. It took a weekend, a single sheet of Baltic birch plywood, and about $50 in materials.

It worked just as well. His therapist approved it. And I thought - every family should be able to do this.

Built For Them is where I share what I've made, with full plans, cut lists, and the kind of detail you actually need to replicate it at home. No experience required beyond basic tools and a few hours.

Built from lived experience

These designs were made for my son first. Then documented so any family can replicate them. That's the whole project.

A note: These plans are a starting point, not a clinical prescription. Always work with your child's OT or PT to confirm a piece of equipment is appropriate for their specific needs before building or using it.
